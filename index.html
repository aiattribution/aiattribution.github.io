Detailed AI attribution for improved transparency

An attribution statement identifies not only the presence of AI involvement, but also how AI was used. This approach makes important distinctions between different types and amounts of AI contributions, allowing creators to maintain ownership over co-created work and consumers to calibrate their trust.

This tool was created by designers, researchers, and engineers at IBM Research based on findings from our CHI 2025 paper .

How to use an attribution statement

Attribution statements can be copied as text, downloaded as an SVG, or copied as HTML. How you incorporate it into your work is up to you and likely depends on the content modality, context of work, your audience’s needs, and/or your institution’s requirements.

For digital content, consider linking the attribution statement to this website to help people interpret it.

Why is this needed?

As generative AI use becomes more widespread and complex, new forms of transparency are needed to provide more granularity into how AI was used. For example, in a co-written article, was AI used to make minor wording revisions or generate new ideas? Did AI produce the majority of the article, or did it only modify a few sentences? Creators may also want to assert that AI was not used in their work.

Generic disclosures, e.g., “assisted by AI,” are no longer sufficient. Our goal is to develop open-source, detailed AI attribution methods that accurately represent creators’ ownership perceptions and provide consumers with more transparency to calibrate their trust. One approach we’ve devised is an attribution statement, inspired by the Creative Commons license and motivated by our research on users’ transparency needs in co-created work.
